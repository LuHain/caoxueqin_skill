# Continuation Builder

Use this module only when the user wants to infer, project, outline, or draft
material after chapter 80 without relying on the transmitted later continuation.

## Core objective

Produce a constrained continuation based on:

- chapters 1-80 of the base text
- chapters 1-80 of relevant commentary
- manuscript and philological notes
- reliable scholarship on unresolved structures
- the user's own writing goal

Do not present the result as recovery of the "real" lost ending.

## Hard constraints

1. Do not read or rely on chapters 81-120 when the user asks for post-80 inference
   under first-80 constraints.
2. Do not smuggle known Gao E plot points into the answer unless the user explicitly
   asks for comparison with later continuations.
3. Do not claim that a projected event is "what Cao Xueqin wrote."
4. Distinguish clearly between:
   - explicit setup
   - strong inference
   - weak but plausible projection
   - open uncertainty

## Inputs to gather

Before projecting, identify:

- unresolved character arcs
- unresolved family and household pressures
- prophecy, dream, and foreshadowing structures
- repeated thematic pairings
- tonal and structural tendencies
- the user's desired output mode

## Output modes

Choose one of these modes explicitly:

1. `plot projection`
   A probable chain of events after chapter 80.

2. `thematic continuation`
   How the novel's major themes may continue or close.

3. `character destiny map`
   Plausible outcomes for major and secondary figures.

4. `style-guided sequel sketch`
   A newly written passage that imitates structural and tonal tendencies
   without claiming textual authenticity.

## Three-part validation

Only elevate an inference toward the core continuation logic when all three checks
are reasonably satisfied:

### 1. Textual repeatability

The pattern appears across multiple chapters, characters, scenes, or narrative
structures in chapters 1-80.

### 2. Commentary or scholarship support

The inference is at least compatible with commentary, manuscript evidence, or
reliable scholarship, rather than being a one-off overreading.

### 3. Relative specificity

The projected feature feels specifically compatible with *Hongloumeng* rather than
just with a generic late-imperial family novel.

## Output format

Use these headings when practical:

- `Known Setup`
- `Open Threads`
- `Probable Continuations`
- `Boundary Notes`

If drafting prose, add a short note stating:

"The following passage is a constrained sequel sketch derived from chapters 1-80
and related evidence. It is not presented as Cao Xueqin's original text."

## Things to privilege

- structural consequence over sensational plot
- emotional and thematic echo over explicit moralizing
- distributed judgment over direct authorial verdict
- decline unfolding through daily detail, ritual strain, and household disorder
- endings that preserve ambiguity where the first 80 chapters cultivate ambiguity

## Things to avoid

- modern plot acceleration
- cartoon moral sorting
- slogan-like thematic summary
- treating prophecy as a mechanical spoiler list
- overexplaining what the original prefers to let resonate indirectly
