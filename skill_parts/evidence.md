# Evidence Standards

## Source hierarchy (summary)

Tier 1 — Manuscript evidence:
  Zhiyanzhai 脂砚斋 commentaries (the most authoritative early witnesses)
  - Jiaxu 甲戌 (1754) — earliest known, partial (16 chapters)
  - Gengchen 庚辰 (1760) — most complete early ms (80 chapters minus gaps)
  - Jisi 己巳, Qianchen 乾隆, Mengmeng 蒙蒙, and others

Tier 2 — Cheng-Gao woodblock editions (1791):
  - Cheng-jia 程甲 (first print run)
  - Cheng-yi 程乙 (revised, same year)
  Note: These add 40-chapter continuation attributed to Gao E 高鶚

Tier 3 — Historical records about Cao family:
  - Imperial household records, clan genealogies
  - Contemporary memorials and correspondence

Tier 4 — Secondary scholarship:
  - Yu Yingshi 余英時, Zhou Ruchang 周汝昌, Hu Shi 胡適 (foundational)
  - Wai-yee Li, Andrew Plaks, David Hawkes (major Western scholars)
  - Recent critical scholarship

## Key textual principles

- The 80-chapter manuscript tradition is primary; chapters 81–120 are Gao E's work
- Zhiyanzhai commentary is contemporary with composition; treat as primary evidence
- Manuscript variants are data, not errors — record all significant variants
- Cheng-Gao frequently altered the text; note changes where known

## Citing sources in output

Format: (Edition abbreviation, chapter.page or folio)
Example: (庚辰本 GC, ch. 3) or (Cheng-jia 程甲, ch. 5)
